Dr. APJ abdul Kalam

The missile man of the india

Here's a time line of Dr.abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931: Born on October 15 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954: Graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1960: Earned a degree in Aeronautical Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1969: Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) from the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • 1970s: Directed groundbreaking projects—Project Devil and Project Valiant.
  • 1982-1983: Served as the chief executive of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP).
  • 1990: Awarded the prestigious Padma Vibhushan.
  • 1992: Appointed Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation.
  • 1997: Honored with the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian award.
  • 2002: Became the 11th President of India, serving until 2007.
  • 2012: Launched the “What Can I Give Movement,” inspiring youth across India.
  • 2015: Passed away due to a cardiac arrest on July 27.

"Dr. Kalam was a true patriot, a great scientist, and an exceptional human being. His contributions to India’s missile program and nuclear capabilities are unparalleled.”
--Dr. Manmohan Singh (Former Prime Minister of India)
