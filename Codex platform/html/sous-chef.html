Ingredients:

  • 200 grams paneer (cubed)
  • 1.5 cups basmati rice
  • 2 large onions (thinly sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • A few strands of saffron
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves (chopped)

Instructions for Preparation:

  1. Rinse the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Soak the saffron strands in warm milk and set aside.
  3. Cooking the Rice: Boil water in a large pot. Add a pinch of salt and a few drops of oil. Add the soaked rice and cook until it‚Äôs 70-80% done. Drain the water and set the rice aside.
  4. Frying Onions: Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Add the sliced onions and fry until they turn golden brown. Remove half of the fried onions and set aside for garnishing.
  5. Cooking Paneer: In the same pan, add cumin seeds, cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, and bay leaf. Saut√© for a minute. Add ginger-garlic paste and saut√© until the raw smell disappears. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft. Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well. Add the paneer cubes and yogurt. Cook for a few minutes until the paneer is well-coated with the spices.
  6. Layering the Biryani: In a heavy-bottomed pot, start layering the biryani. Begin with a layer of cooked rice, followed by the paneer mixture, and then some fried onions. Repeat the layers until all the rice and paneer are used up. Pour the saffron milk over the top layer. Garnish with chopped coriander and mint leaves.
  7. Dum Cooking: Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  8. Serving: Gently fluff the biryani with a fork. Serve hot with raita or a side salad.
