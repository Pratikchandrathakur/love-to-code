AI threats in Cybersecurity

Growing Cybercrime Potential:


As AI advances, so does its potential for misuse in cybercrime. Skilled cybercriminals can leverage AI tools to craft personalized spear-phishing messages that deceive even vigilant employees.
Additionally, deepfake technology powered by AI allows attackers to impersonate high-ranking executives, potentially leading to unauthorized fund transfers or data tampering.


Stories of hacking using AI


AI-Enhanced Phishing Schemes:



Hackers are increasingly using AI-powered chatbots to improve their English-language communication in phishing campaigns.
These chatbots, similar to ChatGPT, help cybercriminals create more convincing messages. By generating grammatically correct and contextually appropriate content, they enhance the success rate of phishing attacks. The National Security Agency (NSA) has observed both criminal and nation-state actors employing such techniques. Financial Deception and Stock Manipulation:



Hackers have used AI to manipulate financial systems. For instance, they create a seemingly valuable stock portfolio using AI-generated information and then cash out before anyone realizes the deceit. In one case, a fake email exchange between company executives discussing how to cover up a financial shortfall was convincingly generated using AI. This demonstrates how AI can be weaponized to impact financial markets and corporate reputations.
